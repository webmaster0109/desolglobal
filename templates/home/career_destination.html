WORK IN POLAND

Did you know that Poland is right in the middle of Europe? That means it's easy to travel around and explore different countries nearby. Poland has really good trains, buses, and trams, so you can get around easily.

One of the best things about Poland is that it's not too expensive to live here. You can find affordable places to stay and eat delicious food without spending too much money

And here's something cool: If you work in Poland for 2 years, you can get a permanent license to live here! And after living here for 5 years, you can even apply for a Polish passport.

Poland is full of history, beautiful nature, and lots of people speak English. It's a great place to start your next adventure!

STUDY OR WORK IN LITHUANIA

Lithuania is your ticket to a great life without breaking the bank. It's one of Europe's most affordable places to live, meaning you can enjoy a high quality of life without spending a fortune. From cheap rent to budget-friendly meals, your money goes a long way here. Renting a one-bedroom apartment in the city center typically ranges from €200 to €400 per month, while enjoying a delicious meal at a local restaurant can cost as little as €5.

But Lithuania isn't just about saving money – it's also packed with history, charm, and natural beauty. With ancient castles, charming old towns, and stunning countryside, there's plenty to explore and enjoy.

And here's a fun fact: Lithuania has some of the fastest internet in the world! Plus, basketball is a big deal here, so you'll fit right in if you're a fan.

Ready to start your adventure in Lithuania? Discover study opportunities that fit your interests and goals, and get ready to experience all that this amazing country has to offer.

WORK IN CZECH

Located right in the middle of Europe, the Czech Republic is a special place to live. Its capital city, Prague, is in the west, so it's easy to travel around Europe from here.

People here enjoy a really good life. It's not too expensive to live here compared to other European countries, which means you can have a nice life without spending too much money.

The Czech Republic is also very safe. You don't have to worry much about crime here – it's one of the safest countries in Europe.

If you're thinking about studying, you're in luck! Education is free, even at universities. And there are lots of programs taught in English, so you can study and enjoy life here without any language barriers.
